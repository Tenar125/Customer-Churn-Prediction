# Customer-Churn-Prediction
Project Title: Bank Customer Analysis & Insights
Description

This project analyzes a bank’s customer dataset to uncover insights about customer behavior, including tenure, balance, credit score, and churn. The analysis aims to identify patterns, trends, and potential factors influencing customer churn.

The project includes:

Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

Data visualization with charts

Observations and insights for decision-making
Dataset

The dataset contains information on bank customers, including:

CustomerID: Unique identifier for each customer

CreditScore: Customer credit score

Geography/Country: Country of the customer

Gender: Male/Female

Age: Customer age

Tenure(in Years): Number of years as a customer

Balance: Account balance

NumOfProducts: Number of products purchased

Key Features

Customer Segmentation: Based on age, credit score, and tenure

Churn Analysis: Identifying active vs inactive customers

Balance Insights: Average balance by credit score and tenure

Visualization: Bar charts, violin plots, and other plots for clear insightsrd: Whether the customer has a credit card

Observations

Customers with a medium credit score have the highest average balance, while high credit score customers have slightly lower average balance.

The largest number of customers have a tenure of 2 years. The count gradually decreases for years 3–5, then slightly rises again in years 7–8.

Active and inactive customer percentages are roughly 48.5% and 51.5%, respectively.

Success rate by job type shows that students have the highest success rate, suggesting potential for targeted investment.
